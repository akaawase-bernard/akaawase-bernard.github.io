I am a second-year Ph.D. student in the Marine Science department at the University of Connecticut, working with Professor Leonel Romero.

My thesis focuses on investigating the bimoldal structure of wind wave spectrum in the context of air-sea interaction. My research interests generally include remote sensing of surface waves, breaking dynamics, turbulence, ocean physics, ocean wave modeling, and computational methods. Here is my CV.

I am originally from Benue, Nigeria, and speak Tiv. In my free time, I enjoy playing violin, soccer and volleyball, making food, and hiking.
